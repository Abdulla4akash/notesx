# COMP64501 — Introduction to Machine Learning: worked practice question bank

**Source used:** the uploaded MHT lecture sheet, `COMP64501 Chapter 1 - Introduction to Machine Learning (1).mht`.

This bank drills only the task types actually covered by the sheet. I have deliberately **not** added methods that the sheet does not teach, such as normal equations, calculus derivations of least squares, k-means updates, density-estimation formulae, or dimensionality-reduction algorithms.

---

## Task types identified from the sheet

The sheet supports the following computational or procedural task types:

1. **Identify the basic ML objects:** feature vector $x$, label $y$, instance $(x,y)$, training set $\{(x_i,y_i)\}_{i=1}^N$, new input $x^*$, predictive function $f(x,w)$, and parameters $w$.
2. **Classify a learning setup:** decide whether a problem is supervised or unsupervised, based on whether labels are available during learning.
3. **Distinguish classification from regression:** if $y$ is discrete, it is classification; if $y$ is continuous, it is regression.
4. **Match unsupervised tasks:** clustering means finding similar groups; density estimation means finding a probability function for $x$; dimensionality reduction/visualisation means finding a lower-dimensional representation for $x$.
5. **Set up and use a simple predictive model:** especially the Olympic 100m linear model

   $$
   y = w_1x + w_0.
   $$

6. **Compute predictions, residuals, squared errors, and total least-squares error:**

   $$
   E(w_0,w_1)=\sum_i (y_i-f(x_i))^2
   =\sum_i [y_i-(w_1x_i+w_0)]^2.
   $$

7. **Interpret training, fitting, and generalisation:** separate fitting the training data from predicting new inputs $x^*$.
8. **Recognise the main ML challenges listed by the sheet:** insufficient training data, nonrepresentative training data, poor-quality data, irrelevant features, overfitting, and underfitting.
9. **Handle edge cases:** cases where labels are ambiguous, output type changes the task, linear extrapolation becomes unreasonable, or the rounded Olympic parameters do not reproduce the slide's displayed prediction exactly.

## Question C2 — Build the least-squares objective

Use the training set

$$
(0,2),\quad (1,3),\quad (2,5)
$$

and the linear model

$$
f(x)=w_1x+w_0.
$$

Write the least-squares objective $E(w_0,w_1)$ explicitly for these three data points.

### Solution C2

**Step 1 — Write the general least-squares objective.**

$$
E(w_0,w_1)=\sum_i [y_i-(w_1x_i+w_0)]^2.
$$

**Step 2 — List the three data points.**

$$
(x_1,y_1)=(0,2),
$$

$$
(x_2,y_2)=(1,3),
$$

$$
(x_3,y_3)=(2,5).
$$

**Step 3 — Substitute the first point.**

$$
[y_1-(w_1x_1+w_0)]^2=[2-(w_1\cdot 0+w_0)]^2.
$$

So the first term is

$$
(2-w_0)^2.
$$

**Step 4 — Substitute the second point.**

$$
[y_2-(w_1x_2+w_0)]^2=[3-(w_1\cdot 1+w_0)]^2.
$$

So the second term is

$$
[3-(w_1+w_0)]^2.
$$

**Step 5 — Substitute the third point.**

$$
[y_3-(w_1x_3+w_0)]^2=[5-(2w_1+w_0)]^2.
$$

**Step 6 — Sum the terms.**

$$
E(w_0,w_1)=(2-w_0)^2+[3-(w_1+w_0)]^2+[5-(2w_1+w_0)]^2.
$$

**Answer:**

$$
\boxed{E(w_0,w_1)=(2-w_0)^2+[3-(w_1+w_0)]^2+[5-(2w_1+w_0)]^2.}
$$

## Question C3 — Evaluate a least-squares objective

Use the same training set:

$$
(0,2),\quad (1,3),\quad (2,5).
$$

Evaluate the total least-squares error for

$$
w_0=2,\qquad w_1=1.5.
$$

### Solution C3

**Step 1 — Write the model.**

$$
f(x)=w_1x+w_0.
$$

With $w_0=2$ and $w_1=1.5$,

$$
f(x)=1.5x+2.
$$

**Step 2 — Compute the prediction for $x=0$.**

$$
f(0)=1.5(0)+2=2.
$$

Residual:

$$
y-f(x)=2-2=0.
$$

Squared error:

$$
0^2=0.
$$

**Step 3 — Compute the prediction for $x=1$.**

$$
f(1)=1.5(1)+2=3.5.
$$

Residual:

$$
3-3.5=-0.5.
$$

Squared error:

$$
(-0.5)^2=0.25.
$$

**Step 4 — Compute the prediction for $x=2$.**

$$
f(2)=1.5(2)+2=5.
$$

Residual:

$$
5-5=0.
$$

Squared error:

$$
0^2=0.
$$

**Step 5 — Sum the squared errors.**

$$
E=0+0.25+0=0.25.
$$

**Answer:** the total least-squares error is

$$
\boxed{E=0.25.}
$$

## Question C4 — Choose the better fitted parameters by least squares

For the training set

$$
(0,1),\quad (1,3),\quad (2,5),
$$

compare these two candidate models:

Model A:

$$
f_A(x)=2x+1.
$$

Model B:

$$
f_B(x)=x+2.

### Solution C4

f_A(0)=1,
$$

$$
f_A(1)=3,
$$

$$
f_A(2)=5.

$$

$$
3-3=0,
$$

$$
5-5=0.
$$

**Step 3 — Compute Model A's total squared error.**

$$
E_A=0^2+0^2+0^2=0.
$$

**Step 4 — Compute Model B's predictions.**

For $f_B(x)=x+2$:

$$
f_B(0)=2,
$$

$$
f_B(1)=3,

## Question M4

For the training set $(0,0)$ and $(1,2)$, write the least-squares objective for $f(x)=w_1x+w_0$.

### Solution M4

**Step 1 — Write the general form.**

$$
E(w_0,w_1)=\sum_i [y_i-(w_1x_i+w_0)]^2.
$$

**Step 2 — Substitute $(0,0)$.**

$$
[0-(w_1\cdot 0+w_0)]^2=(0-w_0)^2.
$$

**Step 3 — Substitute $(1,2)$.**

$$
[2-(w_1\cdot 1+w_0)]^2=[2-(w_1+w_0)]^2.
$$

**Answer:**

$$
E(w_0,w_1)=(0-w_0)^2+[2-(w_1+w_0)]^2.
$$

# What to memorise from this sheet

1. **Core notation:** $x$, $y$, $(x,y)$, $(x_1,y_1),\ldots,(x_N,y_N)$, $x^*$, $f(x,w)$.
2. **Supervised vs unsupervised:** labels available $\Rightarrow$ supervised; only $x_1,\ldots,x_N$ available $\Rightarrow$ unsupervised.
3. **Classification vs regression:** discrete $y$ $\Rightarrow$ classification; continuous $y$ $\Rightarrow$ regression.
4. **Unsupervised task matching:** similar groups $\Rightarrow$ clustering; probability function for $x$ $\Rightarrow$ density estimation; lower-dimensional representation $\Rightarrow$ dimensionality reduction/visualisation.
5. **Olympic linear model:** $y=w_1x+w_0$, where $x$ is year and $y$ is 100m time in seconds.
6. **Least squares:** residual $y_i-f(x_i)$; squared residual $(y_i-f(x_i))^2$; total error $\sum_i (y_i-f(x_i))^2$.
7. **Generalisation:** prediction on new inputs $x^*$, not just fit to training examples.
8. **Olympic 2012 numbers:** slide-stated prediction $9.59$, actual value $9.63$, difference $0.04$ seconds; be aware that exact arithmetic using the rounded displayed coefficients gives a discrepancy.
